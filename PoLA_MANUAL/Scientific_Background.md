PoLA achieves this method described on:
[Porosity Local Analysis (PoLA): A New Approach to Describe the Porous Volume Distribution in Amorphous Carbons ](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acsomega.5c02479)

It discretizes the simulation box into tiny cubic elements (blocks).

They classify each block happening depending on its quadrature from reverse nationwide surfaces (MinD).

For each empty block:

-  multiple directions are scanned
-  distances to the material walls are computed
-  the **minimum distance (MinD)** is assigned

This quantity reflects the confinement of adsorbate molecules at local level and is also a measure of the host–guest interaction potential.

The core output of PoLA is the **V(MinD) distribution**, defined as the porous volume associated with blocks having a given MinD value.
This representation provides a **continuous, local description of porosity**, unlike traditional pore size distributions based on idealized geometries.

From V(MinD), it is possible to:
- identify regions of different confinement
- relate structure to adsorption behavior
- derive cumulative volume distributions
